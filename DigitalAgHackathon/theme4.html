Closing the Loop on Waste & Nutrients

üî¥ The Friction (Why this is hard)

We operate a linear system on a circular planet. We extract nutrients in one place and dump them as "waste" in another.

  • The 30% Problem: 1/3 of all food is wasted .
  • The Distance Problem: Nutrients (Soy) leave the Americas and end up as manure in Asia. The loop is broken.
  • The Heavy Transport Problem: Manure is mostly water. It's too expensive to truck back to the farm.
  • The "Dirty" Resource: Human waste is nutrient-rich but full of pathogens and PFAS.

üéì Learning from Experience: The "High-Tech" Trap

The Trap: High CAPEX. Building massive insect factories (like Ynsect) costs more than the protein is worth.

The Opportunity: Decentralization. Instead of one $100M factory, use small, modular units on 1,000 farms.

The Trap: Biological Volatility. Biology isn't software. One virus can crash a centralized facility.

The Opportunity: Resilience. Distributed networks survive shocks better.

üß© Key Concepts

Circular Economy: Restoration, not "end-of-life." Feed > Fuel > Compost > Landfill.

Nutrient Decoupling: The separation of animals (source) from crops (sink).

Upstream vs Downstream Waste: Developing world wastes at harvest (Upstream); Developed world wastes at retail (Downstream) .
