Will It Actually Work?

Cool tech is not enough. In agriculture, "great ideas" die every day because they don't fit the user's budget or infrastructure. Before you pitch, run your idea through these filters.

1. The Persona Check: "One Size Fits None"

Who are you building for? A solution for an Iowa corn giant will fail a Kenyan cassava farmer.

Who: Farms < 2 hectares (70% of world farms). Family labor .

The Constraint: Risk Aversion. One bad crop means starvation. They cannot afford to "experiment."

Tech Reality: Likely has a feature phone or basic smartphone. No reliable Wi-Fi. No capital.

‚ò†Ô∏è The Kill Criteria: If it requires upfront capital ($$) or high-bandwidth internet, it fails.

üöú Profile B: The Industrial Operator

Who: Farms 1,000+ acres or high-tech greenhouses. Hired labor.

The Constraint: Margin Pressure. They operate on razor-thin profits. Tech must pay for itself fast.

Tech Reality: Has broadband and heavy machinery. But data systems are siloed and proprietary .

‚ò†Ô∏è The Kill Criteria: If it slows down harvest or adds manual data entry, it fails.

2. The Viability Canvas: Who Pays?

A "sustainable" solution that runs out of money is not sustainable.

üí∞ Path A: The Commercial Startup

The Value Prop: "We save the farmer $X per acre" OR "We increase yield by Y%."

The Red Flag (CAPEX): If your solution requires a farmer to buy a $50,000 robot, how many years until it pays for itself?

The Red Flag (Pilotitis): Building an app that works for 6 months and dies when you graduate.
