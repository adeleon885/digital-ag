Welcome, Hackers. We are in an era of rapid change and grand challenges.

  • Global population is expected to reach 10 billion over the next few decades.
  • Agriculture covers one half of the Earth's habitable land (not rock or ice).
  • Food systems account for one third of global greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Agriculture is the sector most vulnerable to climate change.
  • Zoonotic disease pandemics pose increasing threats.
This perfect storm is one of the grand challenges of our time: How do we feed a growing population, in the face of heightened risks, while protecting the environment? Digital agriculture - leveraging artificial intelligence, IoT (Internet of Things), data science, robotics, and novel sensors - offers a promising pathway to foster sustainable farming and food production.

To have real world impact, a project must be able to survive and thrive in the real world. Make sure you have thought about how to make your project viable.

Path A: Commercial

Focus: ROI (return on), Scalability, Market Fit.

"How does this make money or reduce costs for a business?"

Path B: Non-Profit / Social

Focus: Adoption, Resilience, Public Good.

"How does this survive financially and actually get used?"
