Controlled Environment Agriculture: The Indoor Paradox

üî¥ The Friction (Why this is hard)

We can grow food anywhere, but it is currently too expensive and energy-intensive.

  • The Energy Trap: Growing "local" indoors can create more carbon than shipping food if the lights or heating systems burn fossil fuels.
  • The "Dumb" Building: Heaters and vents often fight each other, wasting massive energy.
  • Nutrient Drift: Plants consume ions at different speeds, unbalancing the water and forcing waste.

üéì Learning from Experience: The "Unit Economics" Trap

The Trap: Expensive Light, Cheap Product. Vertical farms (e.g., AppHarvest) failed by spending millions to grow low-margin lettuce.

The Opportunity: High-Value Crops. Focus on crops where quality commands a premium (herbs, pharma).

The Opportunity: Targeted Automation. Don't replace every human. Automate only the bottlenecks.
